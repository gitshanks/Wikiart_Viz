ART OVER THE AGES

Discussion about the evolution of art over the years, the type of art and how art has varied from time to time showing various emotions and how people pursue art.

Evolution

Art has evolved over generations.

When people started painting, the arts were the only form of communication between humans. Over the years, with the development of their life styles, arts are methods of expressing the artist's emotions, imaginations, ideas, and visions. As shown in the graph, there are not many records of arts until 1800. With the time of the industrial revolution, from 1760 to 1850, the arts started becoming popular. After the end of the second world war, around 1950, the number of arts reaches to peak because there were many art movements started.

Paintings

With the advent of technology, new forms of art evolved, but that did not hinder the popularity of painitings.

Let’s break down the types of the arts over the years. The arts can be painting or the others such as sculptures and objects. As shown in the graph, over the years, most of the arts are paintings. Around 1900, artists started creating other than paintings. They started creating sculptures, figures, or art on the streets.

Specifics

Art preference has changed with time and people.

Painting faces has always been popular over the years. In art history, portrait paintings shows powerful status of the rich. Over the years, there are a few arts depicting a body. Interestingly, in the 1900s, instead of drawing face or body, the artists started drawing more like scenery or buildings. 20th century is also known as art movements such as modernism, cubism, futurism. With the invention of camera in 1800s, people preferred pictures of themselves more than a painitng. As the technology got better and better, the trend of a face/body painting has reduced.

Style

Along with the category and preferences, different styles of art has come to light.

Nowadays, people look at art works to see the example of beauties or expand their imaginations. Over the years, there are mainly six styles of the arts. Overall, if the arts are bodies or facies, then they tend to like them regardless of the styles of arts. Interestingly, if the arts are neither body nor face, then the opinions are different. Especially, if the arts are contemporary art or the mixture of contemporary art and modern art, they tend not to like the arts.

Happiness

People have expressed varied expressions for art and here we talk about the happiness.

Besides the styles of the artworks, there might be other factors that contribute to the ratings of art pieces. As shown in the graph, there is a positive correlation between the mean ratings and the happiness score. People would more like art if they’re more evoking happiness. If we take a look at Tennis, we can see the piece shows happiness and people like them (happiness: 1.00, rating: 2.64). On the other hand, Hornet does not show much happiness and its rating is low.(happiness: 0; rating: -1.90)

Sadness

Here we talk about the sadness expressed for a given type of art.

Although happiness showed a strong correlation with the mean ratings, sadness does not strongly correlate with the mean ratings. If we look at the relationships between sadness and mean ratings, the trend line is relatively flat compared to the one on the happiness. It means that there is not much correlation between the two.

Surprise

Surprise is negatively correlated with ratings.

We further compare the other emotions with the ratings. The graph shows the relationship between surprise and ratings. Unlike happiness and sadness, surprise has a negative correlation with ratings. For Nile, people think it’s surprising (0.85) and do not like much (rating: -0.69). If the art piece is less surprising, then people tend to like it, for example, Young mother contemplating her sleeping child in candlelight. (surprise: 0.1; rating: 2.80).





HOW WE WENT ABOUT DESIGNING THIS PROJECT

The Process

Preprocessing And Design

We’ve used the provided dataset that is preprocessed and cleaned. So we did not perform any additional pre-process for this project.


Design Process

Our design process consists of the mainly following steps:

1. An initial brainstorming meeting where we checked the website of the dataset, looked at some art pieces, read about the art history to gain background knowledge.
2. We came up with possible visualizations and sketched some visualizations.
3. Using python script, we computed people's correlations between emotions and mean ratings to see if we can find some interesting.
4. Since we picked option 2, we used Tableau to generate visualization.




Above And Beyond

Style: To keep the style consistent, we use a template downloaded from the One Page Love. Then, we adjusted the style sheet to keep the style consistent across the webpage.

Interesting Tasks: Three visualizations show the correlation between the sentiments and rating values. The plot between the happiness and rating shows that there is a strong correlation. On the other hand, the surprise is negatively correlated with mean rating. They show that the emotion on the arts has some relationships to the ratings. If the arts are happier, then people tend to like them. Some other emotions, like sadness were not related to ratings.

Integrate Imagery: After plotting the correlations between happiness and rating, it would be more insightful if we actually map each data point to the art images so that we can look and compare the arts. To achieve this, we selected a couple of key artworks, then added those hyperlinks to the art info pages. This provided more context to the visualizations.

Coordinated Views: Firstly, we plot the number of art pieces over the years. Then, we found that there is a spike around the year 1960s. We kept explorating the data to see what is the underlying reasons for the spike. We checked if that’s the paining or not. Not paining arts contributed to the spike, but it is not as high as the one we saw in the first plot. Then, we also plot graph depending on face, body, netiehr. As the graph shows, the number of arts that are not face nor body increased that year. One plot with the number of arts over the years helps to see the overall trends of arts. The other plots with specific types of arts help to see how the types of arts changed and what arts they work on.
